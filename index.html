Sherbrooke Street Currently

Rue Sherbrooke is an iconic street that runs across Montreal from east to west, spanning over 31 kilometres. However, getting around on foot or by car is the safest way to navigate the busy street. Despite the lack of bike lanes and many potholes, you'll still see many cyclists peddling along Sherbrooke.

Sherbrooke Street with Bike Lanes

With bike lanes, however, many more people would be able to travel along the street. It also would allow a greater group of people to travel further, like children, and those with disabilities that wouldn't be able to drive. A bike lane like this is affordable and easy to install, with temporary features that could be improved over time.

Sherbrooke Street with Bike and Bus Lanes

By creating a dedicated bus lane, separate from car traffic, buses can travel more quickly through the city. It also wouldn't require buses to pull into bus stops at every stop, which normally would add time to the trip. By making a permanent bus lane, it encourages an increase in frequency of bus routes, which in the end would reduce car traffic. Also, by making the bike lane more protected, that would encourage people newer to biking to use it.
